One or two letter character that is used to identify an element

(A) Atomic mass

(B) atomic number

(C) ** symbol

(D) Isotope

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A chemical symbol is a one-or two-letter designation of an element. Some examples of chemical symbols are O for oxygen, Zn for zinc, and Fe for iron. The first letter of a symbol is always capitalized.

Concept note-2: -Every element is also represented by a chemical symbol, a one or two letter symbol. When the element is represented by two letters, the first letter is always capitalized and the second letter is lowercase. The chemical symbol of hydrogen is H.

Concept note-3: -There are 14 elements represented by a single letter on the periodic table: Hydrogen (H), Boron (B), Carbon (C), Nitrogen (N), Oxygen (O), Fluorine (F), Potassium (K), Yttirium (Y), Iodine (I), Tungsten (W), Uranium (U), Sulfur (S), Phosphorus (P), and Vanadium (V).

Concept note-4: -A pure substance that is made of only one kind of atom. The sybol for and Element is always one or two letters. When the sybol contain two letters the first letter is always capital and the second is always lower case. A pure substance containing two or more elements that are chemically combined.
