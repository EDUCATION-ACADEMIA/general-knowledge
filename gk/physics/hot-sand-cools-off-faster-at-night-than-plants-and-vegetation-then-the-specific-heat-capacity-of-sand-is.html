Hot sand cools off faster at night than plants and vegetation. Then, the specific heat capacity of sand is

(A) ** less than that of plants

(B) more than that of plants

(C) likely the same as that of plants

(D) not enough information to answer

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer and Explanation: Hot sand cools off faster at night than vegetation because the sandâ€™s specific heat capacity is less than the specific heat of vegetation. Specific heat can be defined as the measure of the amount of heat required to raise the temperature by 1-degree celcius per unit of mass.

Concept note-2: -Sand has a low specific heat, as evidenced by its relatively large temperature changes (for small changes in internal energy), i.e. a small thermal inertia.

Concept note-3: -Sand heated up faster than water because sand is darker than water; dark colored materials will absorb more light.

Concept note-4: -Since, specific heat capacity of water is more than that of sand, it takes more time to become hot. For sand, the specific heat capacity is very less, so it heats up quickly during the day.

Concept note-5: -The amount of heat given by the sun to sea water and beach sand is the same on a hot sunny day. However, the sand heats up faster than the water. This demonstrates that the amount of heat required to heat the sand is less. As a result, the specific heat of sand is lower than that of water.
