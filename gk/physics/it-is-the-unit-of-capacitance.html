It is the unit of capacitance.

(A) Ampere

(B) Coulomb

(C) ** Farad

(D) Volts

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A farad (F) is the standard unit of capacitance (C) in the International System of Units (SI). It indicates the ability of a substance to hold an electric charge. The value of most electrical capacitors is expressed in farads, microfarads (µF) or nanofarads (nF).

Concept note-2: -farad, unit of electrical capacitance (ability to hold an electric charge), in the metre–kilogram–second system of physical units, named in honour of the English scientist Michael Faraday. The capacitance of a capacitor is one farad when one coulomb of electricity changes the potential between the plates by one volt.

Concept note-3: -The unit of electrical capacitance is the farad (abbreviated F), named after the English physicist and chemist Michael Faraday. The capacitance C of a capacitor is the ratio of the charge Q stored in the capacitor to the applied dc voltage U: C = Q/U.

Concept note-4: -The farad (symbol: F) is the unit of electrical capacitance, the ability of a body to store an electrical charge, in the International System of Units (SI), equivalent to 1 coulomb per volt (C/V). It is named after the English physicist Michael Faraday (1791–1867). In SI base units 1 F = 1 kg−1⋅m−2⋅s4⋅A2.

Concept note-5: -The unit of capacitance is the farad, (symbol F ).
