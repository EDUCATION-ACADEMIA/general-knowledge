Which of these correctly compares the masses of different objects in the universe?

(A) A moon has less mass than a star and more mass than the planet it orbits.

(B) A planet has less mass than a galaxy and more mass than the star it orbits.

(C) A galaxy has less mass than a moon and more mass than a planet.

(D) ** A star has less mass than a galaxy and more mass than a planet.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Summary • A planet must be found orbiting a star just like Earth orbits around the Sun. Stars have a greater mass than planets. The gravity of all this mass is what “locks” planets into their orbits and makes them go around it when they are created.

Concept note-2: -The biggest single entity that scientists have identified in the universe is a supercluster of galaxies called the Hercules-Corona Borealis Great Wall.
