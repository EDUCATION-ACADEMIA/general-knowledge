In fireworks, the green flame is produced because of

(A) ** barium

(B) sodium

(C) mercury

(D) potassium

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The correct answer is Barium. Metal salts commonly used in firework displays include calcium chloride, sodium nitrate, barium chloride, copper chloride, etc. The green flame produced in fire-works is due to the presence of Barium.

Concept note-2: -Barium chloride (green) is unstable at room temperatures, so barium must be combined with a more stable compound (e.g., chlorinated rubber). In this case, the chlorine is released in the heat of the burning of the pyrotechnic composition, to then form barium chloride and produce the green color.

Concept note-3: -Barium is used to create green colors in fireworks, and it can also help stabilize other volatile elements. Carbon is one of the main components of black powder, which is used as a propellent in fireworks.
