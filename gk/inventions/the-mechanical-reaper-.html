The mechanical reaper ____

(A) ** increased farming production

(B) decreased farming production

(C) made farming more difficult

(D) planted seeds automatically for farmers

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Cyrus Hall McCormick invented the mechanical reaper, which combined all the steps that earlier harvesting machines had performed separately. His time-saving invention allowed farmers to more than double their crop size and spurred innovations in farm machinery.

Concept note-2: -The reaper broke the harvest-labor bottleneck by allowing the farmer “to reap as much as he could sow.” This big step toward automation allowed farms to become larger and more productive.

Concept note-3: -In agriculture, a reaper is a farm machine used in crop cutting. In olden days farmers and field workers used to harvest crops with the help of their hands using a device such as sickle. But the use of reaper has modernized agriculture and saved labor cost. It has also made the process faster.

Concept note-4: -The impact of McCormick’s reaper was profound. Crops could be cut far faster than before, and with fewer farm hands to pay. By some estimates, about 75% of the U.S. labor force was connected to agriculture in 1820; by 1968, that number had dropped to just 5%.
